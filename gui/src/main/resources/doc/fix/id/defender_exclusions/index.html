Removing Exclusions from Windows Defender

Windows defender is a security program that is included for free with Windows. It detects and removes viruses from your computer, but some viruses can "exclude" themselves and avoid Windows Defender's security scans. Some excluded files were found on your computer. These files could be used by a virus to hide from your computer's security programs. To fix this, follow the steps below.

  1. First, click on the start button and then the settings gear.

  2. When the Settings app opens, click on the "Search" box at the top and type in "defender".

    TODO picture

  3. Below the search box, the words "Windows Defender Settings" will appear. Click these words.

    TODO picture

  4. Look for and click on the words "Virus and threat protection" next to a picture of a shield.

    TODO picture

  5. A new window will open with the title "Windows Defender Security Console". Click on the words "Virus & threat protection settings".

    TODO picture

  6. Scroll down until you find the text "Exclusions".

    TODO picture

  7. Click on the blue text that says "Add or remove exclusions".

    TODO picture

  8. You will see the text "Add an exclusions" above a list of file names.

    TODO picture

  9. Click the first item in the list to reveal a button with the caption "Remove".

    TODO picture

  10. Click on the "Remove" button.

    TODO picture

  11. You may see a message that says "Do you want to allow this app to make changes to your device?". If this message appears, then press "Yes". If it does not appear, then skip this step.

    TODO picture

  12. Repeat the previous three steps until there are no more files listed under "Add an exclusion".

    TODO picture

  13. Close the "Windows Defender Security Console" and the Settings app. Congratulations, Windows Defender is now protecting your computer!
